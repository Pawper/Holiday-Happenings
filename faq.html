F.A.Q.

If this document is inaccessible in any way, please let us know by posting in #assistance-and-good-will on the Holiday Happenings Discord (you can tag @admins) or by sending an e-mail to support@holidayhappenings.info. The administrators will follow-up with you within 24 hours. You can also anonymously submit the form on the contact page.

Frequently Asked Qeustions

What is Holiday Happenings?

Holiday Happenings is yearly 25 day event for bringing comfort & joy to those who need it in the TTRPG community. It culminates in this year's edition of the Holiday Happenings zine of TTRPG content. The new edition of the zine will be released for free on December 25. There's the OnTheXDay art challlenge, an Itch game jam, and a Discord where you can submit miscellany and expand on Holiday Happenings content (or just hang out!). Holiday Happenings aims for everyone to feel welcome and represented.

Is Holiday Happenings a business? A non-profit?

Holiday Happenings is not a legal entity. It's been primarily coordinated by Phillip Wessels since 2016.

What can you tell me about the upcoming Kickstarter?

It's going to be to raise funds to pay our safety & sensitivity editors, to provide inclusivity sponsorships, as well as to possibly pay for layout. As a reward, Kickstarter backers will be listed in the Holiday Happenings zine PDF.

Where can I see past editions of the Holiday Happenings zine?

You can find them on our Itch page!

Do I have to use Itch to get the zine?

You can download the zines direct from our website as well. We encourage community participation and feedback on Itch.

Is the zine free? Why isn't it listed as free on Itch?

The zine is free, however the 2017 version and onward are priced as pay-what-you-want on Itch. You can choose to pay $0.

Where is the 2018 edition?

Phillip Wessels, the primary coordinator, was not able to coordinate the project in 2018.

On the itch page, why am I seeing a Happenings in Christmas Village zine for 2016/2017 instead of Holiday Happenings for those years?

Please note that the zine was previously known as Happenings in Christmas Village. We changed the name of the event and the zine to be more inclusive.

Is this year's edition of the zine going to be called Happenings in Christmas Village?

No, this year's edition of the zine will be called Holiday Happenings.

Is Holiday Happenings really for me? I am not seeing diversity in the 2016/2017 editions and some of the content concerns me.

It's the goal of Holiday Happenings to be welcoming and representing of all sorts of folks who are seeking comfort and joy this holiday season. It is a crowdsourced publication and there was a lack of diversity in the small crowd participating in 2016 and 2017. The existing work is being edited for safety and sensitivity, and we are taking steps to bring diversity into the crowd, such as the inclusivity sponsorship.

Where does revenue from the Kickstarter and Itch sales go?

Revenue helps us pay for editors and inclusivity sponsorships. We are considering paying for layout assistance, depending on the volume of 2019 submissions.

Does Holiday Happenings hire artists or writers? If I submit art, games or other content do I get paid?

No. All art and content in Holiday Happenings is to be submitted without the expectation of pay. However, Holiday Happenings does hire safety and sensitivity editors and provide limited inclusivity sponsorships. The ethos of the Holiday Happenings zine is to be an open community collaboration with barriers to participation limited as much as possible.

How do I get an inclusivity sponsorship?

Please tell us about yourself via our contact page or by emailing inclusivity@holidayhappenings.info and express your interest in the inclusivity sponsorship. We also reach out to individuals.

Who does the layout for Holiday Happenings? Do they get paid?

Phillip Wessels has done the layout for free since 2016. Payment for layout will be subject for discussion in the community because Phillip is writing this very thing, knows he has a lot of power and wants it all transparent and above-board. 🙂 We are considering paying for layout assistance, depending on the volume of 2019 submissions.

How do I submit art or miscellany for publication?

Please use #submission-box on the Holiday Happenings Discord. By submitting to this channel you are giving us the right to publish in the upcoming & future editions of the Holiday Happenings zine. If it's desired for publication, we will contact you to verify & accept your submission. Please be sure to provide us a way to do so.

Can I participate in the game jam without submitting for publication in Holiday Happenings?

Yes. You can set a price to the game, put a note on the game's page that it's not for publication in the Holiday Happenings zine, or reply with such when we contact you to verify & accept your submission.

How do I submit games for publication?

Please do so via the Itch game jam, or #submission-box on the Holiday Happenings Discord.

Who does web development for Holiday Happenings? Can I help or raise an issue with the site?

Phillip Wessels has done web development for Holiday Happenings since 2019. The website is open-source and can be found on GitHub.
